Our Programs

Program Structure

A four-week online journey from spark to launch — turning bold ideas into real, finished creations.

Week 1

Idea Generation & Exploration

Kids explore their interests, brainstorm boldly, and learn how curiosity becomes the spark for every great idea worth building.

Week 2

Planning & Design

From sketches to storyboards, kids turn raw ideas into clear plans—learning to map out the steps that take a concept toward reality.

Week 3

Build & Execute

Hands-on creation: kids prototype, test, and iterate—turning their plans into real, tangible products they can hold and show off.

Week 4

Present & Launch

Kids share their finished creations with family, friends, and peers—building confidence and learning what it takes to ship an idea into the world.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Young Creators Accelerator — and if your question isn't here, just send us a message.

What ages is the program for?+
The Young Creators Accelerator is built for kids ages 9–15. Activities, mentorship, and project tracks are tailored so younger and older kids alike are challenged at the right level.
How long is the program and when does it run?+
It's a 4-week summer experience. Kids move through four stages — Ideate, Plan & Design, Build & Execute, and Present & Launch — finishing with a live Demo Day where they showcase their project.
Is the program online or in-person?+
Both. We offer in-person and online options so families can pick what fits their schedule and location. Either way, kids work in small groups with personalized guidance.
Does my child need prior experience or special equipment?+
No prior experience needed — just curiosity. For online participants, a laptop or desktop with a webcam and stable internet is all that's required.
What will my child walk away with?+
A finished project they built end-to-end, plus the confidence and real-world skills that come from shipping it — collaboration, problem-solving, and presenting their work to a live audience on Demo Day.
